What Is Competitive College Cheerleading?

Competitive college cheerleading combines gymnastics, acrobatics, dance, strength training, and teamwork. Unlike sideline cheerleading, competitive teams perform highly choreographed routines that are judged on difficulty, execution, synchronization, and overall performance quality.

Teams often practice several times per week while balancing classes, studying, and other responsibilities. National competitions showcase some of the most advanced stunts and tumbling seen in the sport.

Training and Preparation

Successful college cheerleaders spend countless hours preparing for competition. A typical training program includes:

  1. Physical Conditioning
    • Strength Training
    • Cardio Workouts
    • Flexibility Exercises
  2. Technical Skills
    • Tumbling
    • Partner Stunts
    • Pyramids
    • Basket Tosses
  3. Competition Preparation
    • Routine Cleaning
    • Performance Practice
    • Team Synchronization

Skills Needed to Compete

Competitive cheerleaders must develop a unique combination of athletic and interpersonal skills:

  • Strength
  • Balance
  • Flexibility
  • Coordination
  • Trust
  • Communication
  • Leadership
  • Mental Toughness
